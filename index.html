Why rent, when you can SquaTT?

One third of renters in Los Angeles spend more than half their income on rent. Yet, thousands of bank-owned properties lie vacant across the city.

In certain locales, it is possible to seize a vacant property and claim squatter’s rights -granting you right of adverse possession. In a nutshell, this gives you ownership of the abandoned property simply by taking it over (under certain conditions).

Enter your location, and SquaTTr will find a list of nearby bank-owned properties you could seize.

DISCLAIMER:

SquaTTr is created for educational and entertainment purposes only. We do not encourage users to break into bank-owned properties to claim Squatter’s Rights. In fact, we never even checked local laws to see what they are. Check with an actual lawyer, as local squatting laws may vary.

SquaTTr is currently in prototype mode, and only displays foreclosure properties in Los Angeles county. Furthermore, the data returned in this app is probably outdated. We used a free API that returns results from 2017. We make no claims that the data returned is still accurate.

Breaking and entering into an abandoned property could result in arrest, imprisonment, or even death. Just don’t break into any buildings, OK?
